Lesson Plan 1: Greetings and simple polite words

Objective: Learn basic greetings and polite words used in everyday conversation.

Video Link: Learn Spanish Greetings | English to Spanish | Rock 'N Learn or view in site:

Introduce the words: “Hello", “Good morning”, “Good afternoon”, “Good evening”, “Goodbye”, “How are you?”, “My name is”, “Pleased to meet you”, “Fine, thank you”, “What is your name”, “What’s going on?”, “Nothing”, “Yes”, “No”

Greeting Circle: Students stand in a circle and greet the person next to them using phrases like “Hello", “Good morning”, “Good afternoon”, “Good evening”, “Goodbye”, “How are you?”, “My name is”, “Pleased to meet you”, “Fine, thank you”, “What is your name”, “What’s going on?”, “Nothing”, “Yes”, “No”

Next Steps: Each student repeats the greeting until it goes around the circle. Could do a second round and also use a ball: have child 1 start and say a greeting and question then throw it to child 2 who greets them as well and then asks a question to the next child they decide to throw the ball to and so on (mentioned in website lesson plans)

Introduction (~10mins)

Use flashcards with images or simple scenarios (e.g., someone handing over an item with "Thank you").

Main Activities (~20)
Charades
  1. Each team gets in a line, facing in the same direction.
  2. The child at the end of the line (Child A) will turn around, and a volunteer will show them a picture of a greeting to act out.
  3. When Child A has seen the picture, they will turn back around and tap on the shoulder of the child in front of them (Child B).
  4. Child B will turn around to face Child A, and try to mimic the actions of Child A. Child A cannot talk.
  5. When Child B has understood, they will turn back around and tap on the shoulder of the child in front of them (Child C) and repeat the same action.
  6. The process repeats until the child at the front of the line (Child Z) has seen the action.
  7. Child Z will then make a guess as to what the greeting might be, based on the actions.
  8. Each team will have a different greeting to act out, and the team to guess correctly in the shortest amount of time wins.
  9. When everyone has had a turn, teams will count up their points, and the team with the most points wins.

Lesson Plan 2: Colors

Objective: Introduce basic color vocabulary in English.

Warm-up Activity

Introduction: Start with a brief greeting in English ("Hello, good morning!") followed by a short introduction of the day's topic ("Today, we will learn colors").

Icebreaker: Ask the students to say the name of their favorite color in Spanish.

Intro to Basic Vocab, Topic: COLORS, Words introduced: Red, Blue, Yellow, Green, Orange, Purple, Pink

Click for Color activity (canva)
Classroom objects Flashcards in Colorful Illustrative Creative 3D Style by Eva Winchester

Vid link: Spanish Color song + Learn English and Spanish Colors | Nursery Rhymes by EFlashApps or view in site:

Repetitions: Have the children repeat each color's name multiple times, associating it with the object or card shown. Can quiz them by saying a color in spanish and having them reply the same color in English

Practice through Activities (~15 mins)

Color Hunt: Ask the children to find an object in the room that matches the color you name.

Objective: Reinforce color vocabulary by associating it with real-life objects.

Color Dictionary

Materials: paper & scissors(or white cards), something to draw on(paper/whiteboard), 6-10 colored writing utensils, object to draw from (cup, hat, box, bag, etc).

Preparations: cut out 6-10 cards from paper(or use white cards) and write down the English & Spanish name of a color on the front of each card (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple, pink, brown, black, gray). Put all of the cards into an object to draw from. Then place the paper/whiteboard & colored utensils next to the cards.

How to play

  1. Teachers picks a random student
  2. The chosen student faces away from the class and draws a card. Then they pick the writing utensil to match the color
  3. The student makes a drawing with that color and writes its Spanish name next to it
  4. Then they show the class what they drew and the first child to say the English name of the color gets the card(if no one answers correctly the student reads the answer and puts the card back)
  5. Repeat the steps until all cards have been drawn and given to a student
  6. The student with the most cards wins the game

Can then have students sort the separate colors and hang the drawings up somewhere in the classroom next to the english name for each to help students remember them

Intro to simple sentences (~10 min)

Focus: Form simple sentences like "This is red," "The apple is green."

Pair Work: Have the children point to a colored object in the classroom and say the color in English, practicing with a partner.

Might want to first introduce the verb “is” and explain a bit about pronouns/articles in speech

Review and Recap (~10)

Quiz: Hold up colored flashcards and ask the children to name the colors in English

Reinforcement: Repeat the colors learned, asking students to recall them without visual aids.

End with "Goodbye" in English

Repetitions:Have the children repeat each body part's name multiple times, associating it with the part of their own

Click for diagram
Parts of the Body Printable Worksheet in White Brown Orange Cartoon Style by Eva Winchester

Practice Through Activities (15 minutes)

Activity 1: 'Simon Says': Play "Simon Says" using the new vocabulary (e.g., "Simon says touch your head").

Objective: Reinforce body part vocabulary in a fun, interactive way.

Materials: paper & scissors(or white cards), writing utensil

Preparations: cut out 7 cards from paper(or use white cards) and write the name of a body part(head, arm, leg, hand, foot, eye, ear) in English with Spanish translation on the front of each card. (the cards are an optional part of the game, if the students know the English name for body parts well enough then the cards aren’t needed)

How to play

  1. The students stand up and spread out a couple of feet away from each other and the teacher stands in front of the class.
  2. The teacher then starts the game by calling out “Simon dice tocar tu…” and adds the English name for a body part. “Simon dice tocar tu head” (they also have the option to use the cards)
  3. The students then have to touch that body part. But the rule is the students only do as the teacher says if they say Simon dice(Simon Says) before saying the command. For example, if the teacher had said “tocar tu head” without Simon dice, then the students who touched their head would be eliminated and out of the game. The students who touched the wrong body parts would also be eliminated. (The teacher can also add commands to freeze and dance in between to make the game more fun.)
  4. The teacher continues to give different commands(speeding up each command will make the game more challenging and fun) until there is one student left.
  5. That student wins the game
  6. After playing the game once or twice the students can take turns in the teacher’s place giving the commands for each game while using the cards.

Activity 2: Drawing: Ask children to draw a simple person and label the body parts in English.

Objective: Practice vocab through drawing and writing

Introduction to Simple Sentences (10 minutes)

Focus: Form simple sentences like "This is my hand," "I have two legs."

Pair Work: Have children practice the sentences with a partner, pointing to their own or each other’s body parts. if they are unfamiliar with using “this is my” or “I have” could do an activity showing different animals that also have all of the body parts mentioned and having them see the multiple meanings for what each body stands for not just on a human.

Review and Recap (10 minutes)

Quiz: Point to a body part and ask the children to name it in English (could have each kid pat their “arm, leg, etc.” and have other try to quickly say out what they are patting to make a bit more fun)

Reinforcement: Repeat the body parts, asking students to recall them without pointing

Lesson Plan 5: Animals

Objective: Learn the names of common animals and practice forming simple sentences.

Video Link: Animal Names Song or view in site:

Topic: Animals

Words: Introduce 8 common animals (dog, cat, bird, fish, elephant, lion, cow, rabbit).

Visual Aids: Use pictures of animals to show each as you say the word.

Animal Canva

Repetition: Have the children repeat each animal’s name multiple times.

Main Activities (~15 minutes)
Activity 1: Animal Sounds Game
  • Make an animal sound and have the children guess the animal in English.
  • Objective: Reinforce animal vocabulary in a fun way.
Activity 2: Matching Game
  • Provide cards (Canva link above) with animal pictures and names. Have children match the picture to the name.

Objective: Practice animal recognition and vocabulary.

Activity 3: Animal Tag

Materials: None

Preparations: None

  • The teacher picks two animals (one for the tagger and one for the other players) and explains roles in English and Spanish.
  • Choose one student to be the tagger and the rest as players.
  • For example, the tagger could be a lion, and the players could be elephants or cows.
  • The tagger chases the players. Tagged players sit to the side of the room.
  • The last player standing must call out their animal's name in English before the tagger touches them.
  • If they succeed, they are the next tagger. Otherwise, the tagger wins the round.
  • The teacher continues with new predator and prey roles for the next round.

Introduction to Simple Sentences (~10 minutes)

Focus: Form simple sentences like "This is a dog," "The lion is big."

Pair Work

Have children practice sentences with a partner, using animal pictures or toys. Alternatively, write animal names on post-it notes, place them on students' backs, and have them guess their animal by asking two questions in English.

Review and Recap (~10 minutes)

Quick Quiz: Hold up an animal picture and ask the children to name it in English.

Reinforcement: Go over animal names again, asking students to recall them without visual aids.

Goodbye: End the class with a collective "Goodbye" in English.

Activity 3: Escape the Room: School Supplies Hunt

Objective: Escape the room by finding all the hidden school supplies based on the English words provided.

Materials: Various school supplies (e.g., pencils, erasers, notebooks, rulers, crayons, glue sticks, scissors, etc.), clue cards with English names or hints.

Preparations: Hide supplies around the room and prepare clue cards with names or simple sentences.

How to Play:

  1. Divide students into small teams.
  2. Give each team a clue card with the name of a school supply in English.
  3. Teams search the room to find the item matching the clue.
  4. If correct, they get the next clue; if not, they keep searching.
  5. The first team to find all items and return wins and “escapes” the room.

Example Clues:

  • Pencil: “I use this to write my homework.”
  • Eraser: “I use this to correct my mistakes.”
  • Notebook: “I write my notes in this.”
  • Ruler: “I use this to draw straight lines.”
  • Crayons: “I use these to color my drawings.”

Alternative: Use the English word alone for more advanced students.

Introduction to Simple Sentences (~10 minutes)

Focus: Form simple sentences like "This is a pencil," "I have a book."

Pair Work: Have children practice these sentences with a partner, pointing to objects in the classroom.

Alternative Activity: Write object names on post-it notes, attach them to students' backs, and have peers ask questions to guess the object.

Review and Recap (~10 minutes)

Quick Quiz: Hold up an object and ask the children to name it in English.

Reinforcement: Review all objects learned, asking students to recall without visual aids.

Goodbye: End the class with a collective "Goodbye" in English.

Objective: Learn adjectives and use them to describe everyday objects.

Warm-Up (~5 minutes)

Adjective Introduction: Describe an object in the room (e.g., "This is a big, red book") and have students guess what it is.

Introduction (~10 minutes)

Introducing Adjectives: Big, Small, Red, Blue, Soft, Hard.

Visual Aids: Show objects or pictures and describe them using the new adjectives.

Main Activity (~20 minutes)

Adjective Hunt: Students go around the classroom finding objects that match the adjectives introduced (e.g., find something red, find something soft).

Provide a mix of words, and students sort them into adjectives and non-adjectives.

Activity 2: Sentence Building

Students build sentences using adjectives to describe objects (e.g., "The small, blue ball").

Lesson Plan 8: Nature

Objective: Learn vocabulary related to geographical features like mountains, lakes, and oceans.

Warm-Up (~5 minutes)

Nature Word Association: Say the word "nature", “ocean”, “lake”, “mountain” and other words relating to this.

Introduction (~10 minutes)

Introducing Words: Mountain, Lake, Ocean, River, Valley, Hill.

Visual Aids: Show pictures of each geographical feature while saying the word.

Click to see Canva
Geographical Features Canva by Eva Winchester

Main Activity (~20 minutes)

Story Time: Read a short story or show a video that features mountains, lakes, and oceans. Pause to discuss each word and its context in the story.

Game: Click here to play (Select “Nature 1” or “Nature 2”).

Practice (~15 minutes)

Activity 1: Draw and Label

Students draw a simple landscape featuring a mountain, lake, ocean, etc., and label each feature with the correct word. For extra credit, they could also label them with adjectives, colors, or animals that live in that place!

Activity 2: Word Hunt

Hide word cards around the room, and students find and match them with pictures.

Review and Recap (~10 minutes)

Review: Review the words by having students point to pictures of geographical features and say the word.

Recap: Tie in past animal vocabulary by asking questions like “Where does the cat live?” or asking about other animals, and the students will respond with the type of environment they think the animal would live in.

Recap with a Game: Play "I Spy" using the new words (e.g., "I spy with my little eye, something that is a mountain").

Lesson Plan 9: Weather

Video Link: How's the Weather Song or view in site:

Video Link: Canticos | Weather the Weather / Jarabe Tapatío | Best Nursery Rhyme for Kids | Early Education or view in site:

Objective: Learn vocabulary related to different aspects of nature and weather.

Warm-Up (~5 minutes)

Weather Check: Start the class by asking students about the current weather outside (e.g., "Is it sunny or rainy today?"). Students respond using simple weather words they already know and/or point to a picture that currently describes the type of weather that day.

Introduction (~10 minutes)

Introducing Words: Sunny, Rainy, Cloudy, Windy, Stormy, Snowy.

Visual Aids: Use pictures or short video clips of different weather conditions to illustrate each word.

Click to see Canva
Weather Conditions Canva by Eva Winchester

Main Activity (~20 minutes)

Weather Role-Play: Students act out different weather conditions while others guess the word (e.g., pretending to hold an umbrella for "Rainy").

Nature Walk (indoor/outdoor): If possible, take students on a short walk around the school to observe the weather and nature. They can point out and name what they see using the new words (e.g., "The sky is cloudy"). They can also point out any animals they see and describe the shapes and colors of everything around them, reinforcing previously learned vocabulary.

Practice (~15 minutes)

Weather Matching Game: Provide students with weather picture cards and word cards. They match each picture to the correct word.

Click to see Canva
Weather Matching Canva by Eva Winchester

Sentence Building: Students create sentences describing the weather (e.g., "Today is sunny," "It was rainy yesterday").

Review and Recap (~10 minutes)

Weather Chart: As a class, create a weather chart for the week using the new vocabulary. Each student contributes a sentence about what the weather was like on a particular day. They can also draw a picture of a house or tree during each of the weather conditions on this chart.

Quick Quiz: Show pictures of different weather conditions, and students name them. Recap the importance of understanding weather and how it affects our daily lives.

Goodbye: End the class with a collective "Goodbye" in English.
